Decentralized Oracle

Go online and search blockchain. Everyone is fawning over how blockchain will revolutionize everything blah blah blah...

I will take this opportunity to direct you to Hanna Halaburda's excellent article. She points out that there is an unavoidable problem to decentralizing every last government office: the gateway problem. Simply put, how does a decentralized network collect provably true information from the real world?

How, I ask you, will a decentralized blockchain descended from heaven fix land registry problems? The whole idea is for every node on the network to verify all information... so how on Earth is my computer in Toronto supposed to know who really owns a piece of land in Papua New Guinea? The answer is that it's not possible without forcing me to go down to Papua New Guinea and doing my own ivestigation.

So what exactly can we do? Remember that it is necessary for all nodes on the network to be able to verify information (or at least most of them). So any solution to the gateway problem only exists for information which is already common knowledge, such as the outcome of a sports game.

Incentive-Compatible Feedback Payments

The idea is to put up a question, and let members of the public answer it. For this to work, though, we need to ensure that the people answering the question will act honestly. For this we need incentive-compatible payments. If you know that you will get more rewards for answering honestly, then that is what you will do. 

Centralized Oracles

So we need to have a system where members of the public can cast a vote and get a reward. Well, newsflash, stuff like this has existed for ages. Consider slashdot or stackoverflow, which reward users with reputation/badges. Consider Khan Academy, which rewards good behaviour with "energy points". Consider the thousands of webforums which use a karma/kudos system. You can even consider Wikipedia where there is no reward whatsoever, apart from the esteem of your peers.

This problem is solved.

There is only one hitch: all of the above systems are run on a centralized server (or group of servers) and managed by a single entity. I guess this is some kind of philosophical conundrum or something, and our goal is now to decentralize the operator of the reward system.
